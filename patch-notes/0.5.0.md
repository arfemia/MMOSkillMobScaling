The first public release of MMO Mob Scaling, a standalone open-world mob difficulty-scaling companion to the MMO Skill Tree mod. Open-world mobs now scale to the players around them: a high-power group meets tougher, rarer, affixed enemies, while a lone newcomer is never overwhelmed. This is a first beta and the numbers are still being tuned in-game, but everything is data-driven, so you can retune any of it. Requires MMO Skill Tree 1.5.0+ and Ziggfreed's CommonLib 1.2.0+.

- **New: layered open-world difficulty.** Every hostile mob is scaled to a difficulty resolved from three layers: Hytale's own worldgen ZONE and BIOME floors (a starter gradient ships, Zone 0 through Zone 4), a distance-from-spawn ESCALATION (the deeper frontier is harder AND denser with rare mobs), and the real POWER of the players standing in the region (their full MMO Skill Tree build, not just their level).
- **New: rarity ladder + affixes.** Rare, Epic, Legendary, and a forced Boss tier, each a colored nameplate, an aura tint, stat multipliers, affix slots, bonus XP, and a bonus loot table. Five affixes ride native Hytale effects (Armored, Stalwart, Swift, Vampiric, Freezing). Rolls are deterministic per mob, so a chunk reload reproduces the same mob.
- **New: risk pays.** A scaled kill grants bonus MMO XP (with an underdog bonus for punching above your weight) and pulls extra loot from its tier's drop table.
- **New: two player HUD overlays.** A Zone Difficulty card (local difficulty, a threat tier read relative to you, your own and the group's power, and the friendly in-game zone name) and a Mob Inspector card (look at any mob to see its portrait, name, rarity, scaled difficulty, a live health bar, and its affixes as icon chips). Both toggle and reposition live via `/mobscaling hud`.
- **New: `/mobscaling` admin tools.** `inspect` (report the difficulty inputs at your position), `preset` (switch live between Default, Casual, Hardcore, Playtest), `hud` (tune the overlays), and `purge` (strip scaling residue before uninstalling).
- **Data-driven throughout.** Zone floors, rarities, affixes, drop tables, the difficulty-to-stats curve, and both overlays are all Hytale assets you can retune per file or extend from a content pack; nothing is baked into Java.
